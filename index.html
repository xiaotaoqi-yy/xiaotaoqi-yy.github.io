Hi, I'm Yu Yan and I'm very interested in the robot motion planning, manipulation and industrial automation.

I am working as a postdoctoral research fellow in Prof. Pham Quang Cuong's CRI Group at NTU. I received my PhD in mechanical engineering from Ecole Centrale de Nantes (ECN) in 2014, where my supervisors were Prof. Fouad Bennis and Assoc. Prof. Emilie Poirson. My PhD research focused on the motion planning of rigid objects in the virtual assembly, as well as its integration with users' perception. After graduation, I joined the Geometry and Motion Planning Department of Fraunhofer-Chalmers Centre (FCC) as a guest researcher. While at FCC, I worked on the whole body motion planning of virtual human manikins in the IMMA project. In 2016, I returned to China and enrolled in the development of the motion planning software of a dual-arms mobile humanoid at AUBO (Beijing) Robotics Technology (also Smokie Robotics).
